❆ Event culture

EmblemMascotTorchSouvenir of successful bid

On December 15, 2017, the "Water Cube" of the National Swimming Center, which inherits the elements of the 2008 Olympic Games and will usher in the 2022 Winter Olympic Games, witnessed another important moment in the history of the Olympic Movement: the emblem of the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympic Games "winter dream" and the emblem of the winter Paralympic Games "leap", which were unveiled here

The emblem of the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympic Games takes the Chinese character "winter" as the inspiration source, uses the artistic form of Chinese calligraphy, integrates the profound oriental cultural heritage with the international modern style, presents the new image and new dream of China in the new era, and conveys the new era of China to run the Beijing Winter Olympic Games well, realize the dream of the Winter Olympic Games and realize the goal of "300 million people participating in ice and snow sports", Realize the dream of becoming a sports power, promote the development of world ice and snow sports and make new contributions to the International Olympic movement

The upper part of the emblem shows the shape of skaters, and the lower part shows the heroic posture of skiers. The lines dancing in the middle are smooth and full of rhythm, representing the undulating mountains, venues, ice and snow slides and festival dancing ribbons in the venue, adding a festive visual feeling to the emblem and symbolizing that the Beijing Winter Olympic Games will be held during the Chinese Spring Festival. The emblem is mainly blue, implying dreams and the future, as well as the brightness and purity of ice and snow. Red and yellow are derived from the Chinese national flag and represent the passion, youth and vitality of sports

In the form of "Beijing 2022" font, it absorbs the characteristics of Chinese calligraphy and paper cutting, enhances the cultural connotation and expressiveness of the font, and also reflects the sense of integrity and unity with the emblem graphics
